The sacraments

Efficient signs instituted by Christ to give grace. We prepare the faithful with care and joy.

Baptism

Infants: parents and godparents attend two preparation evenings; baptisms are celebrated on the second Sunday of the month after the 10:30 a.m. Mass.

Adults and children of catechetical age: preparation through RCIA.

First Reconciliation & First Holy Communion

Typically second grade; registration opens each spring. See faith formation.

Confirmation

High school sophomores; two-year preparation with retreats and service. Contact the youth office.

Matrimony

Engaged couples should contact the parish at least six months before a proposed wedding date. You will meet with a priest or deacon, complete a marriage preparation course (e.g. CatholicMarriagePrep.org or diocesan retreat—sample link), and plan the liturgy together.

Anointing of the Sick

Before surgery, during serious illness, or for the elderly—call the parish office. In an emergency, ask the hospital chaplain or any Catholic priest.

Funerals

We walk with families in grief. Contact the funeral home and the parish office; a priest or deacon will help plan the Vigil, Funeral Mass, and committal.
